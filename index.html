Abstract

This paper presents MaskACT, a novel approach for robotic manipulation that leverages masked action transformers to learn complex manipulation behaviors from demonstration data. Unlike traditional imitation learning methods, MaskACT employs a masked prediction objective that enables the model to learn robust manipulation policies by predicting masked action sequences. The proposed architecture integrates visual perception with action prediction, allowing the robot to perform precise manipulation tasks in diverse environments. Experimental results demonstrate that MaskACT achieves superior performance compared to existing methods on standard manipulation benchmarks, showing improved generalization capabilities and robustness to environmental variations. The framework's ability to handle partial observability and learn from limited demonstration data makes it particularly suitable for real-world robotic applications where data collection is expensive and time-consuming.
