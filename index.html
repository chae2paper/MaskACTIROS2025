Abstract

Automating flower thinning for high-value crops such as strawberries is critical for optimizing yield, yet it poses a significant challenge in perceiving target flower that is occluded by the unstructured farm environments. In this paper, MaskACT (Mask Action Chunking with Transformer) is introduced, a refined imitation learning framework that enhances ACT (Action Chunking with Transformer) with a binary mask from a visual tracker to guide the policy’s attention. This approach simplifies the learning problem by providing a clear spatial prior of the target. MaskACT achieved a final thinning success rate of 62%, representing an improvement over the baseline’s 40%. Furthermore, the framework demonstrated higher efficiency, completing the task with an average cycle time of approximately 7 seconds, compared to 9 seconds for the baseline. This study highlights the potential of using target tracking to resolve perceptual ambiguity, thereby improving the robustness and efficiency of imitation learning systems.

Model Architecture

"We propose MaskACT, a masked action transformer that learns robust manipulation policies from demonstration data."

The architecture integrates visual perception with action prediction through a transformer-based framework. MaskACT demonstrates superior performance on manipulation benchmarks, showing improved generalization capabilities and robustness to environmental variations compared to existing imitation learning methods.

Experimental Results

We evaluate MaskACT on standard robotic manipulation benchmarks and compare its performance against existing methods. The results demonstrate the effectiveness of our masked action transformer approach across different tasks and environments.
